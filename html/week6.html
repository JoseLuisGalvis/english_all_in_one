🚀 Week 6: Resumen de la Semana – Gramática en Inglés

In this section, you can find the main grammar topics of the week.

This week we will study the following structures: Wh-words, Present Continuous, Prepositions, and IT Vocabulary.

It is important that you read the explanations before the class and if there is something you cannot understand, you can ask your teacher in class.

1. Wh-words (Question Words)

Las Wh-words son palabras interrogativas que se usan para hacer preguntas específicas y obtener información detallada.
🔹 Lista de Wh-words principales
What - ¿Qué? / ¿Cuál?

Se usa para preguntar sobre cosas, objetos o información.

  • What is your name?
  • What are you doing?
  • What time is it?
  • What do you want to eat?
Who - ¿Quién? / ¿Quiénes?

Se usa para preguntar sobre personas.

  • Who is that person?
  • Who called you?
  • Who are you talking to?
  • Who is your manager?
Where - ¿Dónde? / ¿Adónde?

Se usa para preguntar sobre lugares o ubicaciones.

  • Where do you live?
  • Where is the office?
  • Where are you going?
  • Where did you put my keys?
When - ¿Cuándo?

Se usa para preguntar sobre tiempo o momento.

  • When is your birthday?
  • When does the meeting start?
  • When did you arrive?
  • When will you finish?
Why - ¿Por qué?

Se usa para preguntar sobre razones o causas.

  • Why are you late?
  • Why is the system down?
  • Why did you choose this career?
  • Why don't you like it?
How - ¿Cómo?

Se usa para preguntar sobre la manera, método o condición.

  • How are you?
  • How do you spell that?
  • How does this work?
  • How can I help you?
Which - ¿Cuál? / ¿Cuáles?

Se usa para elegir entre opciones limitadas.

  • Which color do you prefer?
  • Which option is better?
  • Which laptop should I buy?
  • Which one is yours?
Whose - ¿De quién?

Se usa para preguntar sobre posesión.

  • Whose car is this?
  • Whose idea was that?
  • Whose phone is ringing?
  • Whose laptop did you borrow?
🔹 Combinaciones con "How"
Expresión Significado Ejemplo
How much ¿Cuánto? (cantidad no contable) How much does it cost?
How many ¿Cuántos? (cantidad contable) How many people are coming?
How long ¿Cuánto tiempo? How long does it take?
How often ¿Con qué frecuencia? How often do you exercise?
How far ¿Qué tan lejos? How far is the airport?
How old ¿Qué edad? How old are you?
👉 Ejemplo de conversación:

A: Who is working on the new project?

B: Sarah and Tom are working on it.

A: When will they finish?

B: They will finish next Friday.

A: Where are they working from?

B: They are working from the main office.

A: How is the project going?

B: It is going very well so far!

2. Present Continuous

El Present Continuous se usa para hablar de acciones que están ocurriendo en este momento o situaciones temporales.
🔹 Estructura básica
Subject + be (am/is/are) + verb-ing + complement
✅ Afirmativa

Subject + am/is/are + verb-ing

  • • I am working now.
  • • She is studying English.
  • • We are developing a new app.
  • • They are testing the system.
❌ Negativa

Subject + am/is/are + not + verb-ing

  • • I am not working today.
  • • She is not (isn't) coming.
  • • We are not (aren't) listening.
  • • They aren't attending the meeting.
❓ Pregunta

Am/Is/Are + subject + verb-ing?

  • • Are you working now?
  • • Is she coming to the party?
  • • Are they fixing the bug?
  • • Is he debugging the code?
🔹 Reglas para agregar -ing
Regla Verbo base Verbo + ing
Mayoría de verbos: + ing work, talk, read working, talking, reading
Termina en -e: quitar e + ing write, make, come writing, making, coming
Una vocal + consonante: duplicar consonante + ing run, sit, stop running, sitting, stopping
Termina en -ie: cambiar ie por y + ing lie, die, tie lying, dying, tying
🔹 Usos principales
⏰ Acciones en este momento
  • I am writing an email right now.
  • She is talking on the phone.
  • They are having a meeting at the moment.
  • We are deploying the application now.
📅 Situaciones temporales
  • I am learning Python this month.
  • She is working on a special project.
  • They are staying at a hotel temporarily.
  • We are testing the new features this week.
🔄 Cambios y tendencias
  • Technology is changing rapidly.
  • The company is growing fast.
  • AI is becoming more popular.
  • Cybersecurity is improving every day.
📆 Planes futuros definitivos
  • I am meeting the client tomorrow.
  • She is starting her new job next Monday.
  • We are launching the product next week.
  • They are presenting at the conference.
🔹 Verbos que NO se usan en continuo
Stative verbs (verbos de estado) generalmente NO se usan en Present Continuous:
  • Mental states: know, understand, believe, think (opinion), remember
  • Emotions: like, love, hate, want, need, prefer
  • Possession: have (tener), own, belong
  • Senses: see, hear, smell, taste (involuntary)
  • Others: be, seem, appear, contain

❌ I am knowing the answer. → ✅ I know the answer.

👉 Ejemplo de conversación:

A: What are you doing right now?

B: I am working on a new database design.

A: Is anyone helping you with the project?

B: Yes, John is testing the queries and Maria is writing the documentation.

A: When are you finishing?

B: We are planning to finish by Friday.

3. Prepositions (Preposiciones)

Las preposiciones son palabras que muestran la relación entre un sustantivo/pronombre y otras palabras en la oración.
🔹 Preposiciones de Tiempo
AT

Hora específica, momentos del día

  • at 9 o'clock
  • at noon / midnight
  • at night
  • at the moment
  • at Christmas / Easter
ON

Días y fechas específicas

  • on Monday
  • on January 5th
  • on my birthday
  • on the weekend
  • on Christmas Day
IN

Meses, años, siglos, períodos largos

  • in March
  • in 2025
  • in the morning / afternoon / evening
  • in summer
  • in the 21st century
Otras comunes
  • for - duración: for 2 hours
  • since - punto de inicio: since Monday
  • during - durante: during the meeting
  • by - antes de: by Friday
  • until - hasta: until 5 PM
🔹 Preposiciones de Lugar
AT

Punto específico, lugares exactos

  • at the door
  • at the bus stop
  • at home / work / school
  • at the conference
  • at 123 Main Street
ON

Superficies, contacto

  • on the table
  • on the wall
  • on the floor
  • on the second floor
  • on Main Street
IN

Espacios cerrados, áreas generales

  • in the room
  • in the building
  • in Argentina
  • in Buenos Aires
  • in the office
Otras posiciones
  • between - entre dos: between the chairs
  • among - entre varios: among the students
  • next to / beside - al lado de
  • in front of - delante de
  • behind - detrás de
  • under / below - debajo de
  • above / over - encima de
🔹 Preposiciones de Movimiento
Preposición Significado Ejemplo
to Hacia un destino I am going to the office.
into Hacia adentro She walked into the room.
out of Hacia afuera He came out of the building.
through A través de We drove through the tunnel.
across Cruzando Walk across the street.
along A lo largo de They walked along the river.
up / down Arriba / Abajo Go up the stairs.
🔹 Preposiciones comunes en IT
💻 Tecnología
  • work on a project
  • log in to the system
  • connect to the network
  • save in the cloud
  • click on the button
  • access through VPN
  • store data in the database
📧 Comunicación
  • send an email to someone
  • reply to a message
  • talk about the problem
  • discuss with the team
  • communicate through chat
  • share files with colleagues
  • collaborate on documents
👉 Ejemplo de uso combinado:

I work at a tech company in Buenos Aires. Every day, I arrive at the office at 9 AM. My desk is on the third floor, next to the window. I usually work on web development projects. In the afternoon, I have meetings with my team. On Fridays, we present our progress to the manager. I finish work at 6 PM and go to the gym.

👉 Ejemplo de conversación profesional:

A: What are you working on right now?

B: I am debugging some issues in the mobile app. There are a few bugs affecting the performance.

A: Are you using any testing framework?

A: When is the deadline for this task?

B: I need to finish by next Wednesday. We are having a code review on Thursday.

A: Where are you storing the test results?

B: I am uploading them to the cloud storage, in the shared workspace.

📋 Week 6 Summary

We have come to the end of this week's activities. We have revised and studied:
❓ Wh-words

Question words to ask for specific information: who, what, where, when, why, how, which, whose.

⏰ Present Continuous

  • Combine all these topics in conversations and writing
